ASCII

ASCII (American Standard Code for Information Interchange) is a character encoding standard for electronic devices. It represents text in computers etc. It was originally developed from telegraph code. Work on it began in October of 1960. The first standard was published in 1963. It was updated in 1967 and then again in 1986. It encodes 128 characters into 7-bit integers as shown in this table. 95 are printable, including 0-9, a-z, A-Z, and punctuation.
