Octal

Octal functions similarly to binary. However, the math uses base 8 instead of 2.

4
+ 4
10

x x x x
512 64 8 1

Octal was once widely used in computers when 12-, 24-, and 36-bit systems were employed. However, with modern computer platforms shifting towards 16-, 32- and 64-bit words, hexadecimal became more commonly used in programming languages instead.

Although related to computers, octal does not relate to RAM.
